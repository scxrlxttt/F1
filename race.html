This Grand Prix also features a sprint race.

Sprint races are high-speed, shorter-format races held on select weekends to add extra excitement to Formula 1. Typically run on Saturdays, they set the starting grid for Sunday’s main Grand Prix and award a smaller number of championship points to the fastest finishers.

With fewer laps and no margin for error, Sprint races demand precision, aggression, and quick thinking. Drivers battle wheel-to-wheel from start to finish, making every overtake and defensive move count. For teams and fans alike, Sprint weekends offer a thrilling preview of what’s to come in the main race while adding an extra layer of strategy, tension, and unpredictability to the championship.
